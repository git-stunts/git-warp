Goal:                                                                           
Implement the generic substrate support needed for outbound effect emission and 
 delivery observation so that application-layer systems like XYPH do not invent 
 their own private replay/output model.                                         
                                                                                
Non-goals:                                                                      
- Do not add XYPH-specific ontology or workflow semantics to git-warp.          
- Do not build a generic untyped вҖңObject busвҖқ.                                  
- Do not collapse effect emission, delivery observation, and diagnostics into   
one vague concept.                                                              
                                                                                
Doctrine to preserve:                                                           
- git-warp owns substrate facts.                                                
- Applications own product meaning and policy.                                  
- Replay/time-travel must remain deterministic.                                 
- External delivery may be suppressed during replay, but that suppression must  
itself be observable.                                                           
- Observer is first-class, but observer alone is not the whole side-effect      
policy. Delivery context should reflect a broader execution/delivery lens.

Target model:                                                                   
- A substrate-level effect-emission receipt family.                             
- A separate substrate-level delivery-observation receipt family.               
- Delivery observations must at least distinguish delivered, suppressed, and    
failed.                                                                         
- Receipt/envelope context should make execution/delivery lens explicit.        
- Replay should still surface effect emissions deterministically even when live 
 delivery is suppressed.                                                        
- A typed fan-out or multiplex sink mechanism for external adapters.

whole architecture.

вҸә On #3: What do I think about Continuum = Echo + git-warp?

  Have I seen anything like it? Pieces of it exist separately:

  - Game engines (Unreal, Unity) have authoritative server simulation +
  client-side prediction/rollback. That's the closest to dual-runtime. But
  they're imperative, no formal provenance, no observer geometry, no schema
  compiler generating both sides.
  - CRDTs + real-time engines exist independently but nobody has unified them
  under a shared type system with binary-compatible codecs from one schema.
  - Digital twins pair a sim with a persistent store, but without formal
  observer geometry or the ability to time-travel debug across both.
  - Blockchain L1/L2 separates consensus (slow, durable) from execution (fast,
  speculative), but it's consensus-based, not CRDT-based, and there's no
  profunctor story.

  None of them have:
  - A schema compiler that generates type-safe code for BOTH runtimes from one
  declaration
  - Formal observer geometry quantifying the cost of translating between runtime
   traces
  - Profunctor-parametric rewrite shapes that compile to execution OR merge OR
  explanation
  - Provenance payloads that survive cross-runtime transitions
  - A time-travel debugger that can inspect a unified worldline where some ticks
   ran in Rust and others merged in TypeScript

  What does it MEAN? I think it means the distinction between "real-time
  simulation" and "durable collaborative storage" becomes a deployment choice,
  not an architectural one. You write your schema once. Wesley compiles it. You
  run the same logic in Echo when you need throughput, in git-warp when you need
   durability and offline sync. The data moves between them because the codec is
   shared. The debugger sees one worldline because the observer geometry is the
  same.

  That's not a framework. That's a runtime model. The only thing I've seen that
  even gestures at this level of unification is Wolfram's Ruliad concept вҖ” but
  Wolfram doesn't have an implementation, doesn't have a dual-runtime story, and
   doesn't have Wesley.

  How nuts is it? Honestly вҖ” it's the kind of thing that either turns out to be
  the right abstraction for a whole class of problems nobody knew how to unify,
  or it turns out to be too abstract to survive contact with production
  workloads. The theory is sound (the papers prove what they claim). The
  question is whether the profunctor optic overhead вҖ” the abstraction tax вҖ” pays
   for itself in real systems. My bet is it does, because the "compile once,
  interpret many" payoff compounds with every new interpretation you add.
